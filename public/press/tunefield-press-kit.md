# Tunefield — Press Kit

## One-liner

Tunefield is a free, offline tool that turns a DJ's music library into a navigable 3D field — so the tracks that mix well sit next to each other, and your next record is something you *see*, not something you scroll for.

## Short description (≈50 words)

Tunefield analyses every track in your library — BPM, key, mood, energy, danceability — and lays them out as a 3D field of stars where proximity means compatibility. Filter by Camelot key, follow the harmonic edges, and find the next track by looking. Free, offline, open-source, built by a DJ.

## Long description (≈120 words)

Every working DJ owns thousands of tracks they've forgotten they have. Rekordbox, Serato and Traktor are all built around scrolling lists — and lists hide what's adjacent. Tunefield takes a different approach: it analyses your library locally (BPM, Camelot key, LUFS, mood, energy, danceability, vocals, timbre) and renders it as a 3D "neural matrix" — every track a star, positioned by the metrics you choose, connected by edges to the tracks it mixes with. You explore your own collection spatially, spot the harmonic path through a set, and rediscover music you already paid for. It runs entirely on your machine — no account, no cloud, no subscription. The core is free and open-source; a one-time Pro tier adds the full DJ-prep workflow.

## Key facts

- **Category:** Music library exploration + DJ-prep tool
- **Platforms:** macOS, Windows (USB-portable build also runs from a stick)
- **Price:** Free core, forever · Pro €79 lifetime (no subscription)
- **Data:** 100% local and offline — your files never leave your machine
- **Licence:** Core is open-source under GNU AGPL-3.0
- **Status:** Public launch targeted for autumn 2026; beta open now
- **Made by:** Mark Burnett — solo developer, working DJ, Ableton Certified Trainer

## The maker

Tunefield is built solo by **Mark Burnett** — a working DJ, producer and **Ableton Certified Trainer** with 34 years behind the decks. He built Tunefield because the tool he wanted didn't exist: a way to *see* a library instead of scrolling it. Everything about the product reflects that — local-first, no lock-in, honest pricing, open-source at the core.
